Daily Check-In

How is your
heart today?

Take thirty seconds. Put one hand on your chest. Take a breath. Now ask: what is actually in here right now?

Today's questions:

What emotion showed up most strongly in the last 24 hours?

Was there a moment where your heart felt protected or guarded? Why?

What is one thing you are quietly hoping for today?

Proverbs 4:23 — Above all else, guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it.

Journal Prompts

Write your way to clarity

The Psalms are journals. David wrote his raw emotions to God. These prompts invite you to do the same.

For Anger

Lord, I am angry because... and underneath that anger, I think I am actually afraid of...

For Loneliness

The places I feel most alone are... and what I most wish someone would say to me is...

For Joy

When I notice joy, I tend to... and I wonder if that says something about what I truly believe about life...

Self-Reflection

Below the surface

These questions go deeper than behavior — into the beliefs and stories your heart is running on.

What emotion do I find hardest to admit I feel — and why?

What did I learn growing up about how to handle difficult emotions?

When I am at my most reactive, what is the fear underneath the feeling?

What would it mean for my life if I actually believed I was fully known and fully loved?

Guided Exercise

The Lament Practice

A five-minute exercise rooted in the pattern of the Psalms of Lament.

1

Address God honestly

Begin with "God..." and say exactly what you are carrying. No polished words required.

2

Describe the pain

Name what it feels like, what it is costing you, and how long you have been carrying it.

3

Ask the honest question

"How long, God?" or "Why?" — the Psalms show that God receives these questions as prayer, not rebellion.

4

Remember one true thing

End with something you still believe about God — even if it is small. "Yet I will trust." That is enough.
